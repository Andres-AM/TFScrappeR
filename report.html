Summary: The text is a decision by the Swiss Federal Supreme Court (Bundesgericht) on a case involving the Swiss pension system (AHV). The court’s ruling concerns the calculation of an adjustment to pensions that are temporarily suspended (Rentenzuschlag).

The key points are:

  1. The Bundesrat (Federal Council) had introduced a new method for calculating the Rentenzuschlag, which was based on the average value of the suspended pension benefits rather than the pension benefit that would have been payable at the time of suspension.
  2. The court found that this new method was in line with the constitutional and statutory requirements, which aim to ensure that pensions are adjusted to reflect changes in living costs and income.
  3. The court rejected the plaintiff’s argument that the new method was inconsistent with the constitution and statutes, as it would lead to a disproportionate increase in the value of the Rentenzuschlag over time.
  4. The court concluded that the Bundesrat had not exceeded its statutory powers in introducing the new method.
  5. As a result, the court dismissed the appeal and ordered the plaintiff to pay the costs of CHF 500.

The decision was communicated to the parties involved (the pension fund, the Social Insurance Court of Zurich, and the Federal Office for Social Insurance) on December 20, 2023.

Summary: The text is a judgment of the Swiss Federal Supreme Court (Bundesgericht) in a case regarding an invalidity pension. The court rules that the applicant’s claim for an early retirement pension due to disability is not admissible, as she has not demonstrated any objective grounds justifying the expansion of the provision on provisional insurance (provisorische Weiterversicherung) to cases like hers.

The court notes that the legislative intention behind this provision was to address specific obstacles in reintegration attempts for retirees, which are different from those faced by individuals who have not yet retired. The applicant’s situation is characterized by a lower barrier to reintegration and no equivalent need for protection as retirees do.

The court also rejects the argument that the retroactive award of a reduced or time-limited invalidity pension would be subject to the same rules as revising an existing pension claim under Article 17 ATSG. The court finds that there is no constitutional requirement to apply the provision in this case, and therefore upholds the decision of the lower court.

The judgment concludes by awarding the costs of Fr. 800.- to the applicant, and notifying the parties involved with a written copy of the judgment.
